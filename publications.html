Graduate Research · University of Roehampton

Digital Ordering, Perceived Fairness, and Time Transparency

  • Identified that perceived wait time is a sociotechnical construct influenced by transparency, predictability, and environmental cues.
  • Mixed-method research integrating qualitative interviews with descriptive analysis.
  • Redesigned a digital food pickup service and projected a 35% reduction in perceived wait time.
  • Highlighted psychological expectation management in service trust and mapped disconnects between physical service realities and digital representations.
