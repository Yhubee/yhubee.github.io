Teaching, Mentoring, and Leadership

My teaching and mentoring history spans formal instruction, youth development programs, and community engagement initiatives. Working with learners from different backgrounds taught me that autonomy is fragile in the presence of authoritative systems and that explanation style deeply affects comprehension, confidence, and willingness to question.

Mercy Corps – ENGINE Program (Education, Nigeria)

Role: Group Head, Literacy & Life Skills Instructor · Focus: Empowerment, confidence-building, learning scaffolding

Trained more than 100 adolescent girls across literacy, numeracy, and life skills by designing structured learning modules, facilitating small-group study sessions, identifying learners’ confidence gaps, supporting self-efficacy and personal agency, and helping participants navigate educational transitions.

DLCF Learning Program – Benin City

Role: STS Teacher · Focus: Collaborative learning, conceptual grounding, scaffolded instruction

Taught secondary school students in a structured STS (Science, Technology, and Society) learning environment. I learned how learners form misconceptions, how they internalize explanations, and how teaching methods influence cognitive load and decision-making confidence.

Truthsprout Nigeria – Country Director

Focus: Youth empowerment, civic engagement, leadership development

Oversaw national programs focused on digital literacy, personal leadership development, civic responsibility, life-skills training, and community-based mentorship models. This leadership experience strengthens my commitment to developing AI systems that serve real communities, not abstract use cases.

Sustainable Merton – London

Role: Volunteer · Focus: Community sustainability, local engagement

Participated in zero-waste initiatives, community gardening, and food distribution networks. These programs broadened my understanding of public-facing service ecosystems and how people interact with community-support infrastructure—experience that informs my research into human–AI collaboration in public contexts.

How Teaching Informs My Research

  • Autonomy is fragile in the presence of authoritative systems.
  • Explanation style deeply affects comprehension, confidence, and willingness to question.
  • Cultural and cognitive diversity shape how people interpret technology.
  • Users bring emotional, social, and contextual histories into interactions with AI.
  • Guidance systems must protect—not erode—human dignity.
