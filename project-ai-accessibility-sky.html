AI Accessibility Feature for Sky

Exploring how AI-driven personalization can support accessibility while keeping users in control.

Role: Product Management Certificate Capstone

Context: King's College London professional certificate project

Domains: Accessibility, AI Personalization, Inclusive Media Design, Regulation (WCAG, Ofcom)

Methods: Accessibility heuristics, user story mapping, collaborative prototyping, regulatory analysis

← Back to Projects & Case Studies

Overview

In collaboration with Sky, I worked on designing an AI-powered media accessibility feature that adapted content presentation based on user needs while respecting regulatory frameworks. The goal was to personalize without overwhelming users or violating consent boundaries.

What I Did

Mapped accessibility needs

Analyzed needs of users with sensory sensitivities, cognitive differences, and reading or comprehension challenges to ensure the system started from real constraints.

Developed inclusive user stories

Stories emphasized control, comprehension, comfort, transparency and consent, and the ability to override automated adjustments.

Balanced personalization with user autonomy

The key tension was: Can AI personalize without becoming intrusive or paternalistic? I foregrounded user-set preferences, limited unsolicited adjustments, ensured explanations of automated behaviors, and enabled predictable interaction patterns.

Impact & Research Value

This project revealed how accessibility, personalization, and value-sensitive design intersect. It strengthened my interest in cross-cultural accessibility, adaptive AI for diverse cognitive styles, and ethical personalization frameworks.
