Human–Centered AI Researcher • Technical AI Product Manager • HCI Practitioner

Focused on Trust, Autonomy, and Sociotechnical Intelligence

I study how people understand, negotiate, and sometimes relinquish control to intelligent systems—and how AI can be designed to strengthen human autonomy rather than quietly erode it.

My research spans human–AI interaction, trust calibration, adaptive explanation design, and sociotechnical systems, with a focus on education technology and accessibility.

AI often behaves confidently even when its reasoning is opaque, and users—especially those navigating uncertainty—may mistake confidence for correctness.

This observation drives my commitment to building AI systems that are transparent, interpretable, and psychologically supportive.
