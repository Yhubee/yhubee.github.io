Research Agenda

Human–AI interaction, trust calibration, adaptive explanations, and sociotechnical value alignment.

My work examines how AI can be designed to strengthen human autonomy, support transparent decision-making, and reflect human values in cognitively demanding environments.

Core Themes

Human–AI Interaction & Trust Calibration

How humans infer system competence, form mental models, and develop patterns of over-reliance or under-reliance across repeated interactions.

Adaptive Explanation Design

Investigating cognitively adaptive and culturally aware explanations, uncertainty disclosures, and multi-pathway reasoning that support reflection without overload.

Sociotechnical Fairness

Designing AI that respects diverse cultural and contextual realities, with value-sensitive defaults, user-managed settings, and visible system limitations.

Longitudinal Autonomy

Measuring autonomy drift, trust trajectories, and value-alignment stability during long-term system use, especially in education and accessibility contexts.

Applied Research Threads

AI-Guided Learning & Advising

Researching rationale surfacing, limited-knowledge disclosures, user-managed settings, and alternative-pathway explanations for international students.

Autonomy-Preserving Interfaces

Developing value-sensitive defaults, boundary-setting controls, and cues that invite users to reflect on alignment rather than defer to AI confidence.

Transparency Impact Over Time

Studying how explanations, ethical defaults, and recommended pathways affect trust calibration, cognitive fatigue, and reliance over repeated use.

Graduate Research · University of Roehampton

Dissertation on digital ordering, perceived fairness, and time transparency. Identified how perceived wait time is constructed by transparency, predictability, and environmental cues, and proposed a redesigned service blueprint projected to reduce perceived wait by ~35%.

Undergraduate Research · University of Benin

Explored how global economic systems shape development outcomes in Akwa Ibom State, forming a foundation for understanding sociotechnical and socio-economic systems.
