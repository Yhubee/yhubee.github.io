AI Learning Companion for International Students

Decision Support · Explanation Design · Trust Calibration · Digital Learning Ecosystems

Problem

International students face complex educational decisions with uncertainty and high stakes. Digital advising often presents opaque recommendations, leading to over-reliance and erosion of agency.

Approach

Conducted interviews to map uncertainty points, mental models, and expectations of AI guidance. Designed adaptive explanation schemas and autonomy-preserving interaction boundaries.

Interventions

  • Reasoned recommendations and confidence-qualified suggestions
  • Multiple-pathway explanations and limited-knowledge disclosures
  • User-managed control settings and value-sensitive defaults
  • Prompts to double-check requirements and reflect on alignment

Outcomes

  • Greater awareness of system limitations
  • Reduced blind acceptance of recommendations
  • Increased engagement with learning and advising content
  • Early evidence of trust calibration and behavioral clustering

Fast Track Food Pickup Digital Service (MSc Dissertation)

Digital Queuing · Perceived Fairness · Service Transparency · Sociotechnical Systems

Research Problem

Perceived wait time is shaped by transparency, environmental cues, interface feedback, and trust in digital-to-physical handoff. Ambiguity erodes confidence even when service is fast.

Methods

Mixed methods including surveys, interviews, and observational workflow mapping to contextualize digital behaviors within physical service realities.

Findings

  • Time visibility improved perceived fairness
  • Ambiguous cues signaled incompetence
  • Clear sequencing (confirmed → preparing → ready) improved confidence
  • Lack of queue transparency led to distrust

Blueprint

Designed transparent service states, prediction-based wait estimates, consistent feedback signage, and reliability-inspired UX patterns—projected to improve perceived wait time by ~35%.

AI Accessibility Feature for Sky

Accessibility · AI Personalization · Inclusive Media Design · Regulation (WCAG, Ofcom)

Context

Co-designed an AI-powered media accessibility feature that adapts content presentation based on user needs while respecting regulatory frameworks.

Actions

  • Mapped accessibility needs for sensory sensitivities and cognitive differences
  • Developed inclusive user stories emphasizing control, comprehension, and consent
  • Balanced personalization with user-set preferences and limited unsolicited adjustments

Result

Advanced a framework for personalization that remains transparent, overrideable, and regulation-aligned.
